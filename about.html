About the Project

 Mission

We all need more sanctification, but many of us struggle for years to find the path to holiness. Truly, our Lord's words are true when He says that the way is narrow. We may know that He is the Way, but have no idea how faith should take us through Him.

Eyes of Glory is here to help any wandering believer find deeper communion in the wounds of Christ by gazing physically and spiritually at He who is truly Glorious, the Lord and Savior Jesus the Christ. As we look at God enfleshed, we are transformed more and more into His image, participating in the Glroy we find in His eyes.

Here, we want you to move forward on the path of salvation. It is our hope here that many future saints will look back on this project as an enlightening place by the power of the Holy Spirit, the mediator of God's personal presence. 

 Position

Although this page relies heavily on Eastern Orthodox theology and iconography, we come from a protestant background and not everyone here is on the same page all the time.

We are not ecumenical in the pluralistic sense that everyone is right in their own way, but we do recognize the strengths and deep faith represented in many different congregations. However, we are ecumenical in that we hold to all seven of the ecumenical councils as the standard and boundary of the faith for all people in all generations, so we urge you to read them and submit to their authoritative interpretation of the Holy Scriptures.

Author Introduction

 

As a pastoral theology student, my passion is guiding others in the faith while being led myself. As a husband, I am blessed beyond all measure with the joyous foretaste of perfect communion with Christ.

Who am I? My name is Michael Beauchamp, a young man studying pastoral theology at Bob Jones University (partial degrees in Biblical Counseling, B.S. and M.A.). I have recently married the love of my life, Taylor, whom I had been actively pursuing since our junior year of high school.


Why did I choose theology? Some choices seem like things we chose ourselves, but other times it is clear that God has a plan for our lives. Growing up, I always expected to be a chemist like my dad, but in junior high, I had the opportunity to preach for a nursing home. Feeling the prompting of the Holy Spirit and a foolish pride in my ability to write outlines, I volunteered and found myself preparing a sermon on spiritual boldness.

 I was fairly confident still that I was still going to be a chemist when I grew up, but from Sunday until Friday morning, I could not get the idea of ministry out of my head. It all came together in my writing class when my teacher (a strong mentor then and a great friend now) came up behind me, put her hands on my shoulders, and said, "Michael, I feel like God is calling you to be a pastor." For me, that was the nail in the coffin. She had no clue that I had been wrestling with the question all week, but the Holy Spirit led her to confirm that calling to me. Even in bouts of doubts, this sign has held me in that calling ever since.

When I finished high school, I went to Bob Jones and joined the society Basilean where I have made great friends who have pushed and encouraged me in the faith. I have to thank them for their influence as I learn what seeking the kingdom means.


Who is this Taylor? In many ways, Taylor is my strength and encourager. Ever since I met her as a high school freshman, I have been head over heels in love. She is a sweet introvert, incredibly dedicated in everything that she does, and so smart (much better GPA than mine). After 3 years of long distance being at different schools, we were finally married on August 13th, 2021. 


What are we doing now? After our honeymoon, we moved away from our families in Warren, Michigan to Lansing, Michigan where Taylor goes to school. I am finishing my degree online with Bob Jones while working a full-time job while my wife finishes hers in residence. Our family housing apartment on campus is small, but cozy because it is with someone I love so much.


If you want to talk more personally, feel free to contact me.
